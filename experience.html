Experience

Development Associate Intern at Switchboard Inc.

Rita worked as a Development Associate Intern at Swtichboard Inc. from June 2021 to August 2021. During her internship, she evaluated the UX/UI of company's online products and websites. She identified UX problems and provided solutions through mockups and prototypes. On top of that, she conducted user testing to test solutions and identify any further design problems. She worked closely with another development associate intern to create presentations on how to improve Switchboard's portal and website. She worked closely with the CTO of Switchboard to make these design problems a priority in fixing, as Switchboard greatly appreciated and listened to the feedback of their interns.

Design Intern at the Shenandoah Valley Black Heritage Project

Rita worked as a Design Intern at the Shenandoah Valley Black Heritage Project, a historical center in Harrisonburg, Virginia. She is worked on the Roots Run Deep project within the organization in which the goal is to create a vitual walking tour of notable historical sites where the black community in Harrisonburg have had successful businesses, schools, communities, and more. Rita helped with the design needs for the project including building a Weebly website, designing the logo for the project, and designing a brochure booklet. She worked with grad student, Lauren Oakes, who came up with the idea for this project and made the tour become a reality. She also has been helping find ways to get the word out about the project to both the Harrisonburg and JMU community, such as reaching out to The Breeze to do include a story about it in the newspaper. Rita started her internship back in September 2020 and finished in May 2021.

Creative Director of Advertising at The Breeze

Rita started working at The Breeze in January 2020 as an Advertisement Designer where she would help design advertisements for companies who wanted to advertise in the print and online versions of the newspaper. She was then promoted to the Creative Director position in the Fall 2020 semester. As the Creative Director, she organizes the ad layout in the print version of the paper in Adobe InDesign, helps the Advertisement Designer with the creation of new ads, helps hire and train new personnel in the advertising department, and helps with other design needs such as the creation of a media book and new logos. She collaborates with the editors and also sometimes helps with the design of some special pages that are printed in the newspaper. She also does the advertisment layouts for The Breeze's special edition papers such as Homecoming and Spring Sports sections. Rita will continue to hold this position until she graduates in May 2022.
